History of Batangas

Batangas is one of the oldest provinces in the Philippines. Early civilizations thrived along waterways such as the Pansipit River. Archaeological finds—stone tools, ancient pottery, jade ornaments, and trade relics—show active contact with China and neighboring Asian cultures.

The name “Batangas” comes from batang, meaning logs, abundant along the Calumpang River. Historically, the region was called “Kumintang”.

Notable Figures

  • Apolinario Mabini – Brains of the Revolution
  • Marcela Agoncillo – Maker of the Philippine Flag
  • Miguel Malvar – Revolutionary General

Culture & Trivia

Batangas is home to Kapeng Barako, Kumintang traditions, and iconic natural landmarks such as Taal Volcano and Verde Island Passage—earning its reputation as the Land of the Brave.
